The Transformer and BiLSTM were evaluated on the same 5-class split.
The Transformer reached test accuracy 1.0000, while the BiLSTM reached 0.9905.
The absolute accuracy difference is 0.0095 in favour of Transformer.
Transformer validation accuracy peaked at epoch 4.
BiLSTM validation accuracy peaked at epoch 5.
The model with fewer epochs to peak validation accuracy appears to converge faster; in this run that was Transformer.
Average Transformer training time per epoch was 24.45 seconds.
Average BiLSTM training time per epoch was 21.15 seconds.
Per-epoch runtime differs because multi-head attention performs dense token-token interactions, while recurrent models process steps sequentially and can be cheaper for short sequences.
Attention heatmaps in the final encoder layer highlight which tokens influence the [CLS] representation, and they can show focused patterns around topic-indicative words.
When the dataset is very small (around 200-300 articles), a BiLSTM can be more data-efficient and less prone to overfitting.
A Transformer may still win if regularisation and learning-rate scheduling are tuned well, but it usually needs stronger supervision to stabilise.
